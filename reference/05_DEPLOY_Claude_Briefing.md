## WHAT DEPLOY IS

DEPLOY · by MO§ES™ is a standalone strategic agentic deployment platform. It is the full campaign engine — a deep, customizable war room for building, configuring, and executing agentic campaigns across real-world objectives.

**One line:** *You have your players. DEPLOY is the field, the playbook, and the scoreboard.*

DEPLOY is not a game. It uses game logic — sports formations, military doctrine, gaming role compositions — as the interface language for agentic orchestration. The metaphor is the instruction. The output is real work.

## V1 ARCHITECTURE — THREE TABS

### Tab 1 — ROSTER / INVENTORY / SKILLS
Building the unit. All agents, documents, and skills as cards and badges. Drag and drop into formation slots. Squad saves for reuse.

### Tab 2 — OBJECTIVE / FORMATION / STRATEGY
Where unit, objective, and strategy come together. Objective category, formation selection, posture configuration (global + per-slot), win conditions, timeline, rotation triggers.

### Tab 3 — LIVE CAMPAIGNS
Active campaigns listed. Click any campaign → live dashboard with timeline, reports, agent activity. Individual agent conversation threads accessible here.

## POSTURE SYSTEM

Three states. Everything reduces here.

| Posture | Function |
|---|---|
| Scout | Recon. Assess, map, surface intelligence. No action yet. |
| Defense | Protect, consolidate, preserve. Don't lose anything. |
| Offense | Push, expand, create, execute. Move forward. |

Same formation means something completely different under different postures. A Flying V on offense is a marketing blitz. A Flying V on defense is a rapid-response cleanup crew.

Posture operates globally (whole formation) and per-slot (individual agent override).

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES

1. The metaphor is the instruction — formation is not a label, it is the architecture
2. Complexity collapses into posture — Scout, Defense, Offense
3. Players before plays — roster built before formation selected
4. Everything is a video game — feel like a roster screen and playbook, not a config panel
5. Governance is always on — posture and oversight are foundation, not features
