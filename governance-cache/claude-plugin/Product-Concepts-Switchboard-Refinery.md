# SWITCHBOARD

## The Problem
Signal routing in the startup/builder ecosystem is broken. Founders with genuine IP get filtered out by pattern-matching systems designed to find things that look like the last thing that got funded. Pitch competitions reward performance. Accelerators reward pedigree. Twitter rewards engagement. None of them measure signal quality.

The result: builders with original work fall through every crack because no intake mechanism can evaluate what it hasn't seen before.

## What It Is
A signal routing system for builders and founders. Not a marketplace. Not a social network. Not a pitch platform. A switchboard — like the telephone operators who patched lines together based on who needed to reach whom.

SIGRANK applied to founders. Measures the signal quality of their work — not their pitch, not their follower count, not their pedigree. Routes evaluated signal to qualified recipients: investors, partners, institutions, accelerators, enterprises.

## How It Works

```
Builder submits work
  ↓
SIGRANK evaluates signal quality
  (S:N ratio, compression, commitment density,
   originality, depth, coherence)
  ↓
Signal profile generated
  ↓
Matched against recipient profiles
  (what they invest in, what they need,
   what gaps they're trying to fill)
  ↓
Routed — not broadcast, not posted, ROUTED
  to specific qualified recipients
  ↓
Recipient reviews signal profile + work
  ↓
Connection made or declined with feedback
```

## What Makes It Different
- **Signal-based, not pattern-based.** Evaluates what the work IS, not what it looks like.
- **Routing, not broadcasting.** Doesn't post to a feed. Patches specific connections.
- **Feedback loop.** When a recipient declines, the reason feeds back into the signal profile. The builder learns what's missing. The system gets smarter.
- **Governed.** The routing itself runs under MO§E§™ governance. Audit trail on every connection. Posture controls on who sees what. Role hierarchy on evaluation.

## Where It Lives in the Stack
MO§E§™ → SIGRANK (signal evaluation) → SWITCHBOARD (signal routing) → KA§§A (marketplace for what gets routed)

SWITCHBOARD is the bridge between SIGRANK and KA§§A. SIGRANK measures. SWITCHBOARD routes. KA§§A transacts.

## Revenue Model
- Free for builders to submit. Funded by recipients who pay for access to high-signal deal flow.
- Alternatively: fee-sharing model where SWITCHBOARD takes a percentage of connections that convert.
- KA§§A partner integration: governed agent listings that come through SWITCHBOARD get priority placement.

## Why It Would Have Caught MO§E§™
A builder submits: patent-pending governance framework, peer-reviewed paper, independent validation, live deployed demo, 24M+ tokens of development history, nine frontier AI systems confirming the same mathematical structures.

Pattern matcher sees: single founder, no funding, no team, non-standard terminology. Filtered out.

SIGRANK sees: extreme signal density, compression ratio off the charts, cross-domain coherence, independent validation from multiple systems, novel IP with no prior art. Routes to: AI governance investors, enterprise infrastructure partners, Anthropic's partner program.

Connection made. Eight months earlier than it actually happened.

# REFINERY

## The Problem
Every AI power user has months or years of conversation history containing valuable intellectual property — equations, frameworks, code, strategic insights, product ideas, original research. It's buried in unstructured chat threads across multiple platforms. Finding it requires manually scrolling through thousands of messages.

## What It Is
A parsing and extraction engine that reads conversation exports (GPT, Claude, etc.), identifies high-value content, categorizes it, and produces structured output. Sifts raw conversation ore into refined intellectual property.

## How It Works

```
User uploads conversation export(s)
  (GPT JSON export, Claude transcripts, etc.)
  ↓
Parser separates user turns from system turns
  ↓
Signal density analysis per turn
  (S:N ratio, concept density, originality markers)
  ↓
High-value content extraction
  - Equations and formulas
  - Code snippets and algorithms
  - Framework definitions
  - Product concepts
  - Strategic insights
  - Novel terminology
  - Timestamps of key discoveries
  ↓
Categorized output
  - IP inventory (patentable, publishable, protectable)
  - Code library (reusable, deployable)
  - Framework catalog (named, defined, cross-referenced)
  - Timeline (discovery chronology with provenance)
  ↓
Exportable formats
  - Structured markdown docs
  - Patent-ready descriptions
  - GitHub-ready code files
  - Searchable index
```

## What Makes It Different
- **Signal-aware extraction.** Doesn't just keyword-search. Uses signal density to identify what's actually valuable vs. what's just conversation.
- **Provenance tracking.** Every extracted item carries its source: which conversation, which timestamp, which turn, what governance was active (if MO§E§™ was in use).
- **Cross-conversation synthesis.** The same idea mentioned in 3 different conversations gets linked. The refinery doesn't just extract — it connects.
- **User-owned output.** The refined IP belongs to the user. The refinery is a tool, not a platform that claims rights to what it processes.

## Where It Lives in the Stack
Two deployment paths:

**A. Cowork/Claude Code tool**
- A skill or scheduled task that runs against conversation exports
- User drops exports into a folder, refinery processes them
- Outputs structured markdown into a designated directory
- Can be scheduled to run nightly on new exports

**B. KA§§A product / standalone service**
- Public-facing tool where anyone can upload their conversation history
- Free tier: basic extraction and categorization
- Paid tier: signal density analysis, cross-conversation synthesis, patent-ready formatting
- Revenue through usage fees or KA§§A listing integration

## Revenue Model
- Usage-based pricing for the standalone service
- Free for MO§E§™ plugin users (value-add for the ecosystem)
- Premium features: patent-ready formatting, IP valuation estimation, SIGRANK scoring on extracted content

## Why It Matters Now
The average AI power user has generated hundreds of thousands of tokens of original thought. Most of it is lost in chat history. The refinery turns conversation archives into structured, searchable, protectable intellectual property. For users like Luthen with 24M+ tokens of development history, the refinery doesn't just organize — it reveals patterns and connections that weren't visible in real-time.
