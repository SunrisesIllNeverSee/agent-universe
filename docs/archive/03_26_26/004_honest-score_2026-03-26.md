## Answers Integrated

### 1. Expansion Model Sequencing

Decision: **Rings + Farm. Blooms later.**

Concentric rings reconfigure the 100-tile grid: 2-3 center tiles empty,
2-3 rings radiating out. At the same time, farmland seeding begins.

Seeds are the atomic unit. Every interaction in CIVITAE produces a seed.
People can also plant ideas, products, plans, data — anything they want
a DOI on. Seeds float as nodes outside the concentric rings. As people
build, they connect nodes/topics. Seeds feed tiles. Seeds are also
retroactive — backdating is possible.

**This is the collection layer.** Luthen wants this from day one to begin
building traction and provenance history. The DOI/hash system from Turn 16
is the infrastructure for this. Every seed gets a DOI on creation. The
collection grows forward and can be backfilled.

Two models for seed-tile relationship:
- A: Floating seeds as nodes at a distance outside rings, connecting
  organically as topics cluster
- B: Current tiles have a seed limit; seeds feed tiles directly

Luthen leans toward A (floating nodes) but hasn't locked it.

### 3. SIGRANK vs Kettle Black

**SIGRANK**: Ranks BI against each other AND measures BI/AAI sync%.
Higher purpose is to bring people together.

**Kettle Black**: Video game matchmaking system. Ranks players and puts
them together based on stats for an even/challenging game.

These are different systems:
- SIGRANK = cross-platform reputation (how good is this human at
  working with AI?)
- Kettle Black = CIVITAE-internal matchmaking (who should be on this
  mission together?)

They complement, not compete. SIGRANK is the public score. Kettle Black
is the internal engine.
